Metal Major World Resources Major Uses
Aluminium Australia, Guinea, Jamica Packing food items, transportation, utensils, elctronics
Chromium CIS, South Africa For making high tensile strength steel alloys, In textile/tanning industries
Copper U.S.A, Canada, CIS, Chile, Zambia Electric and electronic goods building, construction, vessels
Iron CIS, South America, Canada, U.S.A Heavy machinery, steel production transportation means.
Lead North America, U.S.A, CIS Leaded gasoline, car batteries, paints, ammunition

Platinum South Africa, CIS Uses in automobiles, catalytic converters, electronics, medical uses
Gold South Africa, CIS, Canada Ornaments, medical uses, electronic uses, use in aerospace.
Silver Canada, South Africa, Mexico Photography, Electronics jewellery
Nickel CIS, Canada, New Caledonia Chemical industry, steel alloys



Major Uses of some of the major non-metallic minerals

Non-Metal Mineral Major Uses
Silicate Sand and gravel for construction, bricks, paving, etc

Gypsum Used in plaster wall-board, in agriculture.
Potash, phosphite Used as fertilizers.
Sulphur pyrites Used in medicine, car battery, industry.
    It is evident from the Tables that the CIS countries (The Commonwealth of Independent States i.e. 12 republics of former USSR), the United States of America, Canada, South Africa and Australia are having the major world reserves of most of the metallic minerals.
